Welcome,
Gotham Tech.

This spring, we'll work together to build a school-owned DEEDS instructional model grounded in the needs, strengths, realities, and aspirations of your classrooms, your teachers, and your students. Every step of this process is designed with and for your team. This hub is your home base for the journey.

How We Work

What to Expect From Us

This isn't a consulting engagement where we hand you a binder and wish you luck.

🤝

We Build With You

Your teachers and leaders shape every decision. We bring frameworks, facilitation, and broad experience implementing DEEDS in a range of settings. You bring the context and deep expertise on your school and community context.

🔍

Evidence First

No guessing. Every design choice starts with what we actually see and hear in classrooms, surveys, and conversations, combined with what research tells us about the science of learning and development.

📝

Drafts Before Finals

You'll see our work early and often. We build in feedback loops at every stage so that there are no surprises.

🏆

It's Yours

Everything we create is meant to help the Gotham Tech team be sustainable over time — building internal capacity, shared expectations, and systems that strengthen the work year over year.

The Big Picture

An overview of the co-design process we will use to build the Gotham Tech instructional model across all four phases.

Design Team

Co-Design: Our Workflow

An overview of the process we will use to co-design the Gotham Tech instructional model.

1
Phase 1 · March 2026
Build a Shared Vision
Get to know each other, determine who will work on this project and how, and build a shared vision of where we are now and where we are going.
2a
Phase 2 · April
Design Team Kickoff
Share the Current State Blueprint findings. Align on playbook vision, norms, and working agreements.
2b
Phase 2 · Bi-weekly
Playbook Working Sessions
Co-design DEEDS instructional model components. Review drafts, give feedback, and iterate. Teachers bring classroom reality to every decision.
↻ Repeating
✓
Phase 2 · Late April
Playbook Draft Review
Full design team reviews the Gotham Tech instructional model playbook draft. Final feedback round before delivery.
3
Phase 3 · May
Scope & Sequence Sessions
Translate the playbook into a year-long instructional plan. Map units, projects, and assessments across subjects and grades.
↻ Repeating
4
Phase 4 · June
Professional Learning Planning
Design team co-develops the professional learning plan — sessions, materials, and structures for bringing the Gotham Tech instructional model to full staff.
↻ Repeating
🎯
End of Phase 4
Handoff & Next Steps
PL plan and materials ready for implementation. Internal capacity built. Next steps determined together based on what the school needs.

    Transcend Framework
    Conditions for Innovation
    Conviction
    A deep and sustaining belief in the importance and potential of the work being undertaken that fuels engagement and ensures it is prioritized. Includes conviction in the potential of all young people, in a vision of extraordinary learning, to engage in a community-based process, and through prioritization.
    Clarity
    A comprehensive and crisp understanding of the work ahead that provides direction and a path forward. Includes clarity in the current state, in vision, and in the journey path.
    Capacity
    The support of personnel and time required to successfully carry out the work. Includes capacity in skills and capabilities, in time and bandwidth, and through continuity of people in key roles.
    Coalition
    The investment of a committed group of stakeholders who are helping the work become a sustained success. Includes coalition with students and families, educators and school administrators, district staff, and others within and beyond the community.
    Culture
    Values, norms, and practices that support effective and sustainable innovation and learning for all. Includes a culture of trust and mutual respect, of collective practice, and of learning and innovation.
